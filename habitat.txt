NASA Hunch Project:  Lunar Smart Adaptive Habitat Design with AI Psychological Monitoring for Crew Adaptive Positive Mental Health

GOAL: Create a lunar habitat that uses Psychological Research and AI to monitor and support astronaut mental health, while integrating smart building systems for life support and power.

The problem:
Long lunar missions = isolation, stress, and risk to mental health 
Current habitats: focus on physical safety, not psychological needs 
Need: Adaptive, supportive environments for crew well-being Astronauts face unique psychological challenges: isolation, confinement, and stress. Traditional habitats protect physically, but we must also address mental health for mission success.

Technology suggestions to be integrated into habitat design:
Voice recognition/analysis
Facial scans (pupils - pupillometer)
Behavioral Pattern Analysis (No Cameras)
Monitors: Movement patterns, Social withdrawal or hyper-activity, Changes in daily routines
Records: Door usage, Workstation interaction, Task completion timing
Heart Rate Variability (HRV) Monitoring
Monitors: Stress levels, Emotional regulation, Fatigue and anxiety
Integrated into wristbands
Pressure sensors in sleeping bags
Monitors: Sleep duration, sleep stages, restlessness, Circadian misalignment
Using LED technology, the circadian light panel replicates the light spectrum and day-night rhythm of Earth.
Greenery backgrounds in windows simulating earth like views and sounds

Wrist band features:

Heart Rate & HRV: Optical sensors measure heart rate and Heart Rate Variability; low HRV indicates higher stress, while high HRV reflects relaxation and adaptability.
Electrodermal Activity (EDA): Skin electrodes detect changes in sweat gland activity, a direct indicator of emotional arousal and stress.
Skin Temperature: Wrist sensors track subtle temperature changes linked to the body’s fight-or-flight stress response.
Activity & Sleep Patterns: Motion sensors monitor movement and sleep quality to identify behaviors that contribute to or signal chronic stress.
